Tamales (colombian style)

Colombian tamales are larger and heartier, typically made with a mixture of mass, pork, chicken, vegetables, and sometimes rice, all wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. They are especially popular during Christmas.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups flour mass or precooked cornmeal
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup lard or vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp annatto (achiote) for color
  • 1 lb pork, cut into chunks
  • 1 lb chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 2 potatoes, sliced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt, pepper, cumin to taste
  • Banana leaves (soaked in warm water)

How to cook it

  1. Season the pork and chicken with garlic, onion, cumin, salt, and pepper. Let marinate.
  2. In a bowl, mix mass with broth, lard, and annatto for color to form a dough.
  3. Cut banana leaves into large rectangles, place a layer of dough, then add pork, chicken, rice, potatoes, and carrots on top.
  4. Fold the leaves to seal the tamal, then tie with string.
  5. Steam the tamales for 2-3 hours until fully cooked.
  6. Let them rest before serving, traditionally enjoyed during holidays with hot chocolate or coffee.
